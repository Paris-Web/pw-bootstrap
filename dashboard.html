Le résumé en 140 caractères

The journey of creating a team of agents capable of building production quality, accessible React components from a Figma file.

La description complète

[talk can be either in English or French] I've worked on perfecting a team of agents for weeks at a large company that builds software used by half of Americans.

The AI agents share a charter that enforces high accessibility and usability compliance, and use tools that have fast feedback loops ensuring adherence to WCAG standards (Storybook, axe-core, Chrome devtools). At this time, there are a couple of steps where human input is required (to make a few architectural decisions and for screen reader testing), but aside from that, almost everything else is automated (a11y, docs, code, tests, performance...).

I have yet to write publicly about this, but I showed my setup to a few industry experts who confirmed this is on the edge, and I'd love to share my findings at Paris Web on the week of my bday!

This could be turned into a workshop, as I'm giving a lot of Claude/Cursor workshops right now internally at Intuit and externally (for example, at the CSUN assistive tech conference).
